# Project Alphono 34: Factor Research Guide & DeepSeek Prompts

This document contains the highly detailed structural formatting and specific DeepSeek prompts required to map out the systemic dependencies of Gujarat across major functional sectors. When researching, do not accept surface-level economic praise. Enforce a critical analysis of *vulnerabilities, dependencies, and external supply chains*.

---

## 1. Infrastructure & Logistics
**Domain Overview:** The state relies heavily on its coastline. We need to measure the reliance on external materials for continuous infrastructure building, and the risk of monopolization in port management.
**Key Variables to Evaluate:**
- Port utilization vs. hinterland dependency (how much traffic is just pass-through for northern states?).
- Dependency on external investments for digital/telecom infrastructure.
**DeepSeek Prompt:**
> "Conduct a rigorous structural analysis of Gujarat's infrastructure and logistics sector. Specifically, evaluate its port infrastructure (like Mundra and Kandla). What percentage of the trade volume is reliant on the industrial output of other landlocked northern/central Indian states rather than indigenous production? Furthermore, analyze the state's reliance on specific conglomerates for maintaining its logistics backbone. Provide exact statistical dependencies, vulnerability bottlenecks in its transport corridors, and data on the origins of raw materials (steel/cement) used for its massive infrastructural projects over the last decade."

---

## 2. Energy Grid & Power Supply
**Domain Overview:** Despite renewable growth, the baseload for heavy industry requires massive thermal generation.
**Key Variables to Evaluate:**
- Imported coal density vs. local lignite.
- Natural gas import reliance.
- Central grid interdependence.
**DeepSeek Prompt:**
> "Deliver a comprehensive breakdown of Gujarat's energy dependencies. While the state is known for renewable energy, I need the quantitative statistics on its industrial baseload power generation. Specifically calculate: 1) The percentage of imported coal versus locally mined lignite used in its thermal plants. 2) The exact structural reliance on imported Liquefied Natural Gas (LNG) for its gas-based projects. 3) The state's vulnerability and reliance on the Central Transmission Utility (CTU) for grid stability. Do not provide generalized statements; provide tonnage, megawatts, percentage shares, and historical failure points."

---

## 3. Agriculture & Water Security
**Domain Overview:** Northern/Central Gujarat and Saurashtra face chronic aridity, artificially suppressed by monumental engineering (Narmada).
**Key Variables to Evaluate:**
- Sardar Sarovar Dam (Narmada) single-point-of-failure risk.
- Groundwater depletion rates.
- Fertilizer/Urea supply dependencies.
**DeepSeek Prompt:**
> "Critically analyze the water security and agricultural dependencies of Gujarat. Focus deeply on the Sardar Sarovar Canal network (the Narmada project). Quantify the exact percentage of the state's agricultural acreage and industrial estates (like Sanand) that are critically dependent on this single water supply line. Additionally, provide the latest data on the groundwater depletion crisis in North Gujarat, the state's systemic reliance on externally procured subsidized fertilizers (urea/DAP), and the predicted collapse scenarios if the Narmada supply line faces a multi-year drought."

---

## 4. Governance & Fiscal Dependencies
**Domain Overview:** The political economy of the state relies heavily on specific central-state fiscal dynamics.
**Key Variables to Evaluate:**
- Capex funded by central subsidies/grants.
- Debt-to-GDP ratio trends.
- Governance centralization risks.
**DeepSeek Prompt:**
> "Analyze the fiscal and governance dependencies of the Gujarat State Government. Detail the structural reliance on the Central government's devolution of taxes, specifically comparing historical grants versus state-generated revenue streams. Evaluate the state's off-budget borrowings and debt-to-GDP trajectory over the last 15 years. Furthermore, analyze any structural vulnerabilities arising from bureaucratic centralization or reliance on specific policy corridors that favor a few mega-industries over decentralized economic governance."

---

## 5. Demographics, Population & Labor
**Domain Overview:** The state's specialized manufacturing (diamonds, textiles, ceramics) is completely dependent on immigrant labor.
**Key Variables to Evaluate:**
- Percentage of migrant workforce in key districts (Surat, Morbi).
- Demographic aging of the native population vs. youth influx.
- Societal stratification due to diaspora/NRI influence.
**DeepSeek Prompt:**
> "Provide a highly detailed demographic and labor dependency report for Gujarat's primary manufacturing hubs. Quantify the absolute dependency of Surat's diamond polishing and textile sectors, and Morbi's ceramic industry, on out-of-state migrant labor (specifically highlighting states like UP, Bihar, and Odisha). What is the exact percentage of migrant workforce supporting these multi-billion dollar industries? Additionally, analyze the socio-economic padding provided by the Gujarati diaspora (NRIs) in terms of remittances and real-estate investment, and calculate how this external capital velocity artificially inflates the state's local economy."

## 6. Industrial Raw Materials (The Processing Trap)
**Domain Overview:** Gujarat processes, but does not dig. The petrochemicals, pharmaceuticals, and manufacturing sectors rely on raw materials imported from other states/countries.
**Key Variables to Evaluate:**
- Crude oil import reliance for Jamnagar/Dahej refineries.
- Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient (API) imports (from China/elsewhere).
- Minerals/ores from eastern India.
**DeepSeek Prompt:**
> "Investigate Gujarat's position as a 'processing state' rather than a resource-rich state. Deliver concrete statistics on its reliance on external supply chains for raw materials. Specifically measure: 1) The percentage of crude oil imported for its mega-refineries (like Jamnagar) versus domestic supply. 2) The reliance of its massive pharmaceutical sector on imported Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients (APIs), particularly from China. 3) The logistics dependency on acquiring raw minerals (iron ore, bauxite) from Eastern Indian states to feed its manufacturing plants. Provide hard data on how a disruption in these external supply lines would crash the state's industrial output."
