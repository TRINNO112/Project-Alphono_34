## 1. Port Infrastructure Evaluation

### Mundra Port
Mundra, operated by Adani Ports, is **India's largest private commercial port**, handling **over 155 million tonnes per annum (MTPA)** of cargo. It functions as a multipurpose deep-draft port with dedicated terminals for containers, coal, crude, and automobiles. The port hosts SEZs, refineries, and logistics hubs, with sustainability initiatives including solar and wind energy integration .

### Kandla (Deendayal) Port
Kandla remains **one of India's major government ports**, specializing in liquid cargo (oil and petroleum), grains, and chemicals, handling **over 115 MTPA**. It serves as a backbone of India's crude oil imports and plays a pivotal role in Gujarat's strategic energy logistics .

### Statewide Port Capacity
Gujarat's ports collectively handle **nearly 40% of India's total cargo volume**, positioning the state as India's maritime trade gateway . Other significant ports include:
- **Dahej Port**: 20 MTPA capacity, specializing in bulk handling with conveyor systems minimizing dust pollution 
- **Hazira Port**: A deep-water, all-weather port serving South Gujarat, connected to the Delhi-Mumbai Industrial Corridor 
- **Pipavav Port**: 1.35 million TEUs annual container capacity, operated by APM Terminals

## 4. Vulnerability Bottlenecks in Transport Corridors

### Delhi-Mumbai Expressway - 87 km Stretch Delay
The most significant current bottleneck is an **87-km stretch in South Gujarat** (part of the Vadodara-Virar section) where construction is **less than 20% complete**. The dispute between NHAI and contractor Renaissance Infrastructure has pushed the full expressway completion to **FY 2027-28**, despite most of the 1,386 km corridor being ready by June 2026.

**Impact**: Vehicles must exit near Ankleshwar/Bharuch, use the congested National Highway 48, then re-enter the expressway, negating the promised 12-hour Delhi-Mumbai travel time .

### Ghodbunder Road Congestion
Ghodbunder Road serves as a critical freight artery connecting Thane, Mira-Bhayandar, Vasai, and Gujarat-bound freight movement with industrial clusters in Bhiwandi and Nashik. The corridor suffers from:
- Deteriorating road surfaces
- Mixed traffic of freight and passenger vehicles
- Inconsistent enforcement of heavy vehicle restrictions
- Single breakdowns causing entire corridor halts

**Expert recommendation**: Segregation of freight and passenger traffic, expanded lane capacity in ghat sections, and intelligent traffic management systems .

### Bridge Infrastructure Vulnerability
The **Tapi River bridge near Kamrej on National Highway 48** required urgent month-long repairs after a critical expansion joint gap was discovered. The closure was prompted by the tragic Gambhira bridge collapse that claimed 18 lives, highlighting systemic infrastructure aging issues.

The **Rangsetu bridge near Shinor** (built 2005) was closed to heavy vehicles after multiple costly repairs, with a **₹250 crore** new bridge proposed .

## 5. Raw Materials for Infrastructure Projects (Steel/Cement)

### Steel Slag Road Initiative
India's **first all-steel slag road** was inaugurated in Surat in June 2022:
- **1 km six-lane road** constructed using approximately **1 lakh tonnes** of processed steel slag
- Steel slag sourced from **ArcelorMittal Nippon Steel India's Hazira manufacturing plant** (9 MTPA capacity generating 2 MTPA of steel slag)
- Developed with CSIR-Central Road Research Institute (CRRI) technological guidance
- The road handles **1,000-1,200 heavy commercial vehicles daily** transporting goods to Hazira port 

ArcelorMittal Nippon Steel has since received an order from GR Infra Projects for **1 lakh tonnes of steel slag** for the Ena-to-Kim section of the Vadodara-Mumbai Expressway .

## 6. State-Led Infrastructure Planning Response

Recognizing existing vulnerabilities, Gujarat is finalizing a **statewide Logistics Master Plan** covering eight cities: Ahmedabad, Gandhinagar, Vadodara, Surat, Rajkot, Bhavnagar, Jamnagar, and Junagadh.

Key initiatives include:
- **Unified Logistics Interface Platform (ULIP)** database for digital integration of logistics information
- **Gujarat Infrastructure Development Board (GIDB)** monitoring projects exceeding ₹500 crore
- **₹500 crore** investment in city-level logistics masterplans
- Alignment with **PM Gati Shakti** and **Sagarmala Programme** (279 projects nationwide, with 114 port-rail links completed)
